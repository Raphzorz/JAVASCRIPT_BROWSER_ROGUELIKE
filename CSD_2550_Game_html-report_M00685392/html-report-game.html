A basic game that the user can play and obtain a score in

The basic features of the game are listed below

  1. -> The main goal of the game is to kill monsters, level up and reach higher floors
  2. -> Whenever the player touches a portal he travels to the next floor. Every floor increases the stats of spawned monsters
  3. -> The player's score that is logged in the hi-scores is the maximum map(floor level) reached
  4. -> The player may attack with a close range melee attack that uses no mana or else with different spells
  5. -> The player has 4 different spells that he may cycle through. Each has different mana requirements and does different damage
  6. -> Enemies have a chance of dropping different types of potions which give the player permanent stat increases
  7. -> The player may heal by picking up potions or by using the heal spell
  8. -> The player may level up an infinite amount of times
  9. -> The player must find gold across the maps
  10. -> Gold is used to level up abilities upon death
  11. -> Upon death the player starts from map(floor) level 1 but retains his experience gains, gold and levelled up stats
  12. -> The player may view instructions for the game by clicking the "view instructions" button in the main-menu
  13. -> A dragon spawns every 5th floor with increased stats and drops

Instances where advanced JavaScript such as Objects and Arrays are used

Creating Attacks - game.js line 515

An attack group/object is created. Different parameters are given to the attack group such as animations, rate of attack, range and physics. Every time the player or creatures attack an instance is created of this object. Different parameters are applied to this instance depending on the type of attack performed. For example a spell will have different animations play. Having one group for all attacks simplifies the code needed to code attacks.

Generating the game world - game.js line 1391

The game world is split into a large array of squares. Every square is 32x32 pixels. The array is looped through and each of these grid squares is shuffled inside of the array and re-assigned randomly, thus giving them random indices. When placing objects such as trees inside of the world map the locations to which they are assigned will be the indexes of these random grid squares inside of the array.

Creating enemies - game.js line 741

An enemies object is first created. Every created creature is an instantiation of this object. The random math class is used to determine what type of creature will be instantiated. Different creatures have different animations and stats assigned. Enemies are placed randomly throughout the world map

Cycling through spells - game.js, line 1195

An array holds the names of the different unlocked spells the player owns. This is found inside of game.js. Whenever the player presses the spacebar a loop will go through the array and find the name of the spell name at the next index. The spell name will be matched inside of the if statements and the image will be changed to the appropriate spell. The spell cost and mana cost of the spell will also change. An if statement at the bottom of the code block ensures that once the end of the array is reached, the loop will start at the beginning once again.

Creating dynamic status bars - game.js line 1240

The health/mana/xp bars work by creating rectangles which are cropped by a certain percentage of its maximum length. The percentage is determined through simple division. For example to figure out the player's current health and apply that to the health bar the equation (in pseudocode) "(Player health/Max player health) *(maximum rectangle length)" would be applied.

Different sound effects - game.js - lines 1035-1050

Different music is used for the main memnu, shop menu and gameplay. Different sound effects are used depending on what momnstter is hit by an attack. Different sound effects are used for melee attacks and spells. Different sound effects are used depending on whether coins or a potion is picked up. A sound effect plays when the player levels up

Different floors - game.js - line 604

Every time the player finds a portal and goes through it the player advances to a new randomly generated floor. The stats of every monster increase, thus increasing the difficulty of the game and making it harder for the player to reach higher floors and thus higher scores

Tooltips - game.js - lines 581-589

Whenever the player or a monster suffers damage the damage is shown on top of the character/monster and fades away slowly. Whenever the player opens a chest the gold obtained also shows up as a tooltip. When the player levels up a tooltip is shown congratulating the player on levelling up

Different player and monster stats - game.js, used throughout

The player has Wisdom, Strength, Vitality, Speed, maximum mana, health and mana while monsters have strength, speed, health, vitality, and exp they give on death. Strength determines the melee damage,attack speed, splash damage and attack range.Wisdom determines mana regeneration, health healed by healing spells, spell damage and spell size.Speed determines movement and attack speed.Vitality determines the player's maximum health

Randomised terrain creation - game.js, lines 39-58

Every time the player advances a floor the terrrain may be generated as a desert, a snowy area, a forest, wastelands, cracked earth or cracked desert

The possibility of rain game.js, lines 162-186

Every time a map is created there is a 30% chance of it raining

Limitless progression feature - shop.js

Whenever the player dies he may spend gold to increase his stats. Every level increases the gold cost of that skill. Upon resurrecting the player starts again from Floor 1 but retains his experience gains, gold and stat gains

Ability to mute the game - game.js, lines 1079-1105

The player may mute and unmute the game at any time by clicking the mute button

What third party libraries did you use?

The third party library used is shown below

Phaser

The phaser website:https://phaser.io/. Phaser is a 2D game framework. It is a library with pre-created functions that are often used inside of game development. The main features used are the following; Phaser was used to preload assets to ensure smooth running of the game. The framework was used to apply physics to objects, apply collision to objects, add animations to sprites, manipulate the camera and create emitters when the player levels up or whenever a projectile hits an enemy.
